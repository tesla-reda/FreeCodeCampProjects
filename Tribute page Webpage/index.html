Sebastião Ribeiro Salgado

The legendary Brazilian photographer

Sebastião Ribeiro Salgado Júnior is a Brazilian social documentary photographer and photojournalist.

Here's a time line of Sebastião Salgado's life:

  • 1944 - Born in Aimorés, in the state of Minas Gerais, Brazil.
  • 1973 - He chose to abandon a career as an economist and switched to photography.
  • 1979 - he joined the international cooperative of photographers Magnum Photos.
  • 1990 - Lélia and Sebastião have worked since the 1990s on the restoration of a small part of the Atlantic Forest in Brazil.
  • 1994 - he and with his wife Lélia Wanick Salgado formed his own agency, Amazonas Images, in Paris, to represent his work.
  • 1998 - they succeeded in turning their land (Atlantic Forest) into a nature reserve and created the Instituto Terra. The institute is dedicated to a mission of reforestation, conservation and environmental education.
  • 2001 - He has been a UNICEF Goodwill Ambassador.
  • 2004 - Salgado worked on Genesis, aiming at the presentation of the unblemished faces of nature and humanity.
  • 2007 - Salgado displayed his photographs of coffee workers from India, Guatemala, Ethiopia and Brazil at the Brazilian Embassy in London.
  • 2014 - Salgado and his work are the focus of the film The Salt of the Earth, directed by Wim Wenders and Salgado's son, Juliano Ribeiro Salgado.
  • 2015 - The film won a special award at Cannes Film Festival and was nominated for the best Documentary Feature at the 2015 Academy Awards.
"I am a former economist. I never went to photography school to learn photography."

--Sebastião Ribeiro Salgado.
